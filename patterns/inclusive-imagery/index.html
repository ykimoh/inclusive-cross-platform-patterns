Inclusive imagery

Source and audit image content so the people in the product look like the people using the product.

Problem

Stock photography skews toward a narrow demographic, young, white, conventionally able-bodied, conventionally attractive. Illustration sets default to one body type, one skin tone, one assumed gender expression. Users not represented in product imagery don’t see themselves and infer they’re not the audience, even when they are.

The failure mode is double: under-representation says “you’re not here,” and token inclusion (one disabled person in a 12-image grid; one wheelchair stock photo) says “you’re a category, not a person.”

Pattern

  • Source from libraries that are explicitly inclusive: Disabled And Here, Affect the Verb, CreateHER Stock, Nappy.co. Not as a substitute for general libraries, alongside them.
  • Audit each set of imagery against five axes: age, gender expression, race, ability (visible and non-visible), body type. Any axis with zero representation is a flag, not a balance.
  • Avoid token inclusion. One disabled person across an entire product reads as performative. Aim for representation that survives random sampling.
  • Match imagery to the actual user base, not aspirational marketing. If your users are 60% over 40 and the imagery is 100% under 30, the imagery is wrong.
  • Don’t pair imagery and copy that conflict. A photo of one person with copy “guys, look at this” double-fails.

Visual examples

Visual examples (a strip from inclusive libraries paired against a typical generic-stock baseline; an audit grid showing the five-axis check applied to a real product set) will be added during pattern review.

Code

The code surface is small, image semantics and color-correct rendering. Most of the pattern lives in sourcing and review, not in markup.

<!-- Meaningful image: alt text describes what the image conveys, not what it depicts -->
<img
  src="customers-using-product.jpg"
  alt="Three customers reviewing the dashboard together"
>

<!-- Decorative imagery: empty alt; never omitted -->
<img src="brand-flourish.svg" alt="">

<!-- Color-managed rendering for skin tones across displays -->
<picture>
  <source srcset="portrait.avif" type="image/avif">
  <source srcset="portrait.webp" type="image/webp">
  <img src="portrait.jpg" alt="..." loading="lazy">
</picture>

Annotation

Tag each image slot in the design with required-representation criteria, sourcing notes, and the alt text. The criteria drive the asset request; the sourcing note is auditable later.

Image slot: hero/3-up
Required: 3 figures; minimum 2 of 5 axes represented per figure
Source:   Disabled And Here / commission illustration
Alt:      "Three product users in a working session"

WCAG mapping

  • 1.1.1 Non-text Content (opens in new tab) A alt text for image semantics

Inclusive imagery is primarily a content-design pattern. WCAG covers perception of the image (alt text); it does not cover who is in the image. The latter is the inclusion layer this pattern adds.
